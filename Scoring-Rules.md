### Scoring Rules
1. Before the tournament, players predict the scores for each of the 72 group stage matches. Players are awarded 3 points for a correct result (ie win, loss or draw) and 5 points for a correct scoreline. Example: if France beat Albania 3-0, a player receives 0 points for predicting a 1-1 draw, 3 points for predicting a 2-1 France win and 5 points for predicting a 3-0 France win.
2. After the group stage, players predict the scores for the 16 round of 32 matches. After that stage, players will predict scores for the round of 16. After the round of 16, players predict the scores for the four quarterfinals, and so on until the final. Players are still awarded 3 points for a correct result (ie win, loss or draw) and 5 points for a correct scoreline. If a match goes to penalties, the score at the end of extra time is taken as the final score.
3. Before the tournament, players predict 16 teams which will make the second round, 8 teams which will make the quarterfinals, 4 teams which will make the semifinals, the 2 teams that will make the final and the winning team. Players are awarded points as follows:
- 5 points for every correct team in the second round
- 10 points for every correct team in the quarterfinals
- 15 points for every correct team in the semifinals
- 20 points for each correct team in the final
- 25 points for correctly predicting the winning team
(NB Your predictions of which teams go through to each round do not have to be logically consistent with your score predictions in rule 1 - eg you can predict England will lose all their group stage games, but still predict that they'll reach the semifinals if you like)
4. Before the tournament, players predict the outcome of the following bonus categories: top goalscorer, player with the most assists, total number of goals scored, total number of red cards, and the referee who officiates the final. Players are awarded bonus points as follows:
- 15 points for predicting the top goalscorer (including if he finishes tied first)
- 15 points for predicting the player with the most assists (including if he finishes tied first)
- 20 points for predicting the correct number of goals scored; 10 points for predictions that are up to 5 goals higher or up to 5 goals lower than the total
- 10 points for predicting the correct number of red cards; 5 points for predictions that are up to 1 card higher or 1 card lower than the total
- 5 points for predicting which referee will officiate the final
